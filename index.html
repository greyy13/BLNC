Gender, Peer Pressure, and Investment Behavior

How Peer Pressure Affects Men and Women

The result from the previous study shows that Male entrepreneurs from Lipa City are affected by their peers when it comes to investment decision-making. With a mean score of 3.03, shows that male entrepreneurs are more likely to base their decisions on their peers than female entrepreneurs.

This result contrasts with the study of Damong and Perez (2024), who noted that women generally show greater sensitivity to external influences, such as social interactions and peer opinions, especially when making financial decisions. However, the current findings show the opposite pattern, with men being more affected by peer pressure. This contradicts the current result by showing that men may rely more on peer validation and shared experiences in their financial choices, which may explain their higher vulnerability to peer pressure when it comes to investment decision-making.
