What Drives Lipa Entrepreneurs?

Social Media as the Strongest Influence

Social media plays a crucial role in shaping how entrepreneurs in Lipa City make financial and investment decisions. With a high influence score of 3.31, platforms like Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube have become key sources of financial trends, advice, and market insights. Entrepreneurs actively exchange ideas, seek tips, and learn from their peers online—proving that digital connectivity fuels financial growth.

“Social media doesn’t just inform — it transforms how Lipa’s entrepreneurs invest.”

Advice from Entrepreneurs:

The key to realizing a dream is to focus not on success but on significance — and then even the small steps and little victories along your path will take on greater meaning.

“Oprah Winfrey ”

Connection to Study:

This aligns with the finding that social influence and shared purpose online help shape how entrepreneurs perceive and act on financial opportunities.

Gender, Peer Pressure, and Investment Behavior

How Peer Pressure Affects Men and Women

Peer pressure affects men and women differently, showing a statistically significant difference (p = 0.004) in their investment decision-making behaviors.

● Female Entrepreneurs (Mean = 3.04):

More responsive to social cues and peer validation, women often consider group feedback or the success stories of others before making investment decisions. This pattern highlights a collaborative approach to finance, emphasizing trust, mutual reassurance, and continuous learning through interaction and shared experiences.

● Male Entrepreneurs (Mean = 2.86):

Men generally approach investment decisions with greater independence, basing their choices on personal judgment, experience, and analytical reasoning. Although they acknowledge the insights gained from peers, such opinions are often viewed as supplementary information rather than decisive factors. This pattern highlights a self-reliant financial mindset rooted in confidence and strategic thinking.


This gender gap demonstrates that women build investment confidence through collaboration and social interaction, while men derive confidence from independence, personal analysis, and rational evaluation of financial choices.

Key Insight:

“Peer influence serves as both a motivator and a mirror—encouraging women to build assurance through collaboration and shared learning, while motivating men to rely on independence and strategic judgment.”

Gender Sensitivity Note:

Encouraging open financial discussions in gender-balanced peer groups can help both men and women make confident, well-informed decisions while preventing gender inequality in investment involvement

Socio-Economic Sensitivity and Expert Opinion

How Expert Guidance Reflects Social Standing

The study revealed a significant difference in expert opinion influence (p = 0.021) when grouped according to perceived socio-economic status.

● High Socio-Economic Class (Mean = 3.44):

Entrepreneurs in higher socio-economic groups are more likely to consult professional financial experts and mentors before making investment decisions. With greater access to formal training, advisory services, and business networks, they use expert opinions to refine strategies, manage risk, and pursue long-term financial stability. This group’s reliance on expert advice reflects both confidence and access—two advantages closely tied to financial privilege.

● Middle Class (Mean = 3.26):

The majority of respondents belong to the middle socio-economic class. These entrepreneurs typically balance expert recommendations with independent research and peer input. They tend to rely on a mix of online financial resources, informal mentoring, and self-study to make informed choices. This blended approach highlights adaptability and determination to bridge the gap between limited access and informed investing.

● Lower Class (Mean = 3.14):

Entrepreneurs in lower socio-economic groups often depend on personal experience, family insights, or peer networks when making investment decisions. Limited access to expert consultation and financial education can make professional advice less attainable. However, this group demonstrates initiative by seeking knowledge from community connections and free digital platforms, proving that financial awareness can grow even in resource-constrained environments.

Key Insight:

Financial expertise amplifies privilege — but equitable access to expert advice can level the investment playing field.

Socio-Economic Sensitivity Note:

To make financial inclusion a reality, expert guidance should be easily accessible, inexpensive, and customized to the specific needs of entrepreneurs from all walks of life. When professional guidance is distributed equally, it boosts confidence, lowers knowledge barriers, and promotes better, more inclusive investing decisions.

Confidence is Capital

Investment Self-Efficacy Builds Success

Entrepreneurs in Lipa City display strong confidence in their ability to make informed investment decisions, reflected by a mean score of 3.23 (High).

Those who actively pursue financial education, skills training, and continuous learning show even greater discipline when managing risks. Their confidence allows them to stay resilient during market uncertainties—turning knowledge and preparation into strategic advantage. This mindset demonstrates that confidence, when paired with education, is one of the most valuable forms of entrepreneurial capital.

Entrepreneurs who educate themselves before investing are more likely to make strategic, long-term financial choices.

Connection to Study:

The study reinforces this perspective by finding that confidence and perseverance are key components of investing self-efficacy, both of which are necessary characteristics for Lipa City entrepreneurs. Those who believe in themselves and follow their goals despite market hurdles are more likely to make smart, well-informed investing decisions.

Risk Perception Encourages Balanced Decisions

The study revealed that entrepreneurs have a high awareness of risk with a mean of 3.28, showing that they neither avoid nor blindly chase risky ventures. Those with more experience are better at assessing risks and turning them into strategic opportunities. Understanding risk empowers entrepreneurs to stay cautious yet courageous — balancing ambition with careful judgment.

Awareness of risk is not fear — it’s wisdom in action.

Advice from Entrepreneurs:

Connection to Study:

Bridging Social Influence and Financial Success

Significant Relationships and Mediation Effects

The results showed a significant relationship between social influences and investment decision-making (p < 0.001). More importantly, investment self-efficacy and risk perception were found to significantly mediate this relationship. This means that entrepreneurs with strong confidence and risk awareness turn social exposure into effective financial actions.
